I am an aspiring applied statistician and a PhD candidate in the Department of Biostatistics at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health, advised by Dr. Kasper Hansen and Dr. Elizabeth Stuart. In my dissertation I am developing methods to analyze data from genomics and stratified (precision) medicine.

I'm originally from Melbourne, Australia, where I worked in the laboratory of Tony Papenfuss after completing undergraduate and masters degrees in statistics at the University of Melbourne.

My broader statistical interests are in multilevel and causal models, the interplay of bayesian and sampling theory ideas, and effective statistical communication (including graphics and teaching).
