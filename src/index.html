I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow working with Prof. Katherine Lee in the Clinical Epidemiology and Biostatistics Unit at the Murdoch Childrens Research Institute in Melbourne, Australia.

I'm working on the design and analysis of adaptive platform trials—clinical trials that investigate multiple interventions for a disease or condition, with interventions entering or leaving the study as evidence accrues about which ones work best. In particular I'm helping to design an adaptive trial in pediatric sepsis.

Broader research interests

I'm interested helping biomedical scientists generate clear, compelling evidence from experiments:

  • More efficient experimental designs using Bayesian and decision-theoretic ideas.
  • Going beyond subgroup analysis for understanding varying treatment effects in clinical trials.
  • Methods for analyzing experiments in high-throughput biology.
  • Improving statistical communication, including graphics, teaching, and statistical terminology.
Other interests include
  • The interplay of evidence (likelihood and bayesian modelling) and robustness (frequentist reliability and testing; model checks).
  • Graphical models for causality and decision-making.
  • Reproducible research using Nix and related technologies.

Background

I completed my PhD in the Department of Biostatistics at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health, advised by Dr. Kasper Hansen and Prof. Elizabeth Stuart. In my dissertation I developed methods to help tackle problems in molecular biology and targeted (precision) medicine. You can read it here:

  • Chapter 2 proposes simple rank-based statistics for understanding the "overlap" among related conditions based on genomic data.
  • Chapter 3 develops the proportional interaction model, a parsimonious model for understanding how treatment effects vary in clinical trials and other experiments.
I was also part of efforts to pool data from clinical trials of drugs repurposed to treat COVID-19.

Before my PhD I worked in the laboratory of Dr. Tony Papenfuss and completed undergraduate and masters degrees at the University of Melbourne.
